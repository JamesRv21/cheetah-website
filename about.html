Cheetahs are highly vocal animals and use various sounds to communicate with each other.

  • Chirping: A high-pitched call used by mothers to communicate with cubs or for siblings to locate each other.
  • Purring: Cheetahs purr when resting, often as a sign of contentmentâ€”much like house cats.
  • Growling and Hissing: These are warning sounds, often used when threatened or competing for food.
  • Yawns and Ear Movements: These non-vocal signals help express mood or intent.
  • To Other Species: Cheetahs do not intentionally communicate with other species, but growls, hisses, and body language may act as defensive signals against predators or competitors.
