Fun Facts About Cheetahs

  • Speed Demons: Cheetahs are the fastest land animals, capable of reaching speeds up to 70 mph (112 km/h) in short bursts.
  • Zero to Hero: They can go from 0 to 60 mph in just under 3 seconds—faster than most sports cars.
  • Silent Hunters: Cheetahs do not roar like lions or tigers—they chirp, hiss, growl, and purr!
  • Built for Speed: Their claws are non-retractable, acting like cleats for extra traction while running.
  • Tail Rudder: A cheetah’s tail acts like a rudder, helping them balance and steer while chasing prey at high speed.
  • Black “Tear Marks”: The dark lines under their eyes reduce glare from the sun—like natural sunglasses.
  • Fast but Fragile: Despite their speed, cheetahs aren’t strong fighters—they often lose their food to lions or hyenas.
  • Super Sight: Cheetahs hunt by sight, not smell. They spot prey from over 3 miles away!
  • Solo Sprinters: Unlike lions, cheetahs are solitary or live in small family groups called “coalitions.”
  • Low Numbers: There are fewer than 7,000 wild cheetahs left in the world today.
  • Fastest Acceleration: Cheetahs can reach full speed in just a few strides—about three seconds.
  • High Heart Rate: Their heart beats over 250 times per minute during a sprint!
