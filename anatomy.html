Animal Anatomy and Biology

Size Information

  • Male Height: 84–94 cm at the shoulder
  • Female Height: Slightly smaller, ~70–80 cm at the shoulder
  • Male Weight: 45–65 kg
  • Female Weight: 35–45 kg
  • Body Length: 110–150 cm (excluding tail)

Unique Physical Features

Cheetahs have a slim build, long legs, small collar bones, and flexible spines that allow them to stretch their bodies while sprinting. Their non-retractable claws provide traction, and black "tear marks" beneath their eyes help reduce sun glare.

Respiratory System

Cheetahs breathe through lungs like all mammals. During a sprint, their breathing rate can increase dramatically to over 150 breaths per minute to meet oxygen demands.

Lifespan

In the wild, cheetahs live an average of 10–12 years. In captivity, with veterinary care, they can live up to 17 years.
