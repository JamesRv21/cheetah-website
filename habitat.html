Animal Habitat Information

Cheetahs are native to Africa, particularly the sub-Saharan region. Small populations also exist in Iran.

  • Where are cheetahs found? Mostly in Namibia, Botswana, South Africa, and parts of East Africa.
  • Preferred Surroundings: Open grasslands, savannahs, and semi-arid environments with good visibility for hunting.
  • Where do they make their home? Cheetahs do not build permanent homes. They shelter under bushes or in tall grasses, especially when raising cubs.
  • Home Type: Other – typically ground-level nesting spots concealed by vegetation.
